If something happens to Nick
Emily's complete guide · Keep this somewhere safe
Start at the top and work down. You don't need to do everything at once — the first week is about stability, not perfection. You have more financial security than you realize.
First 72 hours — do these first
1
Call Nick's HR / benefits office
Report the death to trigger life insurance and employer death benefits. Ask about: life insurance payout, 401k beneficiary process, unpaid bonus or RSU acceleration, health insurance continuation (COBRA).
2
Get 10+ certified death certificates
You'll need these for every account, insurance claim, and legal process. Order more than you think — each institution wants an original. Funeral home handles this.
3
Don't make any big financial decisions yet
You will receive large sums of money. Do not invest, move, or spend anything significant in the first 30-90 days. Park everything in Capital One savings temporarily. Good decisions come after the fog lifts.
4
Notify our estate attorney
They will guide probate (if needed), trust activation, and asset transfer. Contact info in the contacts section below. Bring a death certificate.
First 30 days
5
File life insurance claims
Nick has life insurance through Principal (employer policy). Call Principal with a death certificate. Payout typically takes 2-4 weeks.
6
Transfer investment accounts
Fidelity 401k (Nick) — call Fidelity to initiate spousal rollover to your own IRA. Do NOT cash out — huge tax hit. Vanguard brokerage — joint account, already yours. MSFT ESPP — call Fidelity, transfer or sell remaining shares.
7
Continue sinking funds — they're pre-funded
All sinking funds (vacation, house, camp, kids' cars, college) are pre-funded at Capital One and Vanguard. Don't touch the principal — just continue existing auto-transfers while you get organized.
8
File for Social Security survivor benefits
You and the kids may be eligible for survivor benefits based on Nick's earnings. Call SSA (1-800-772-1213) or visit ssa.gov. Don't skip this — it can be meaningful monthly income.
